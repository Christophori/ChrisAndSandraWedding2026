The Tale Begins

Our Story

✦

Their tale began, as many good tales do, in an unassuming place—a small bagel shop in Moscow, Idaho. There they spoke long together of learning, of distant lands, and adventures they wished to have. That very day, they set out walking beneath open skies toward the arboretum, and though the road proved longer than expected and twilight gathered softly around them, neither wished to turn back. For in one another they had found something rare and quietly wonderful. Christopher saw in Sandra a keen mind, a gentle strength, and a kindness that seemed to shine without effort. And Sandra saw in Chris one who met her as an equal. One who was kind of heart, keen of mind, and quick with laughter. From that day forward, their paths did not part.

Their bond was forged not in a single moment, but across many seasons. Through years of study, long journeys, and the trials and triumphs of building their lives, they grew together—supporting one another through graduate school, new cities, and new callings. They wandered the wild places of the earth—through the peaks of Banff, the fires of Hawaii, the ancient lands of Ireland and Iceland, and beyond. At last, on a quiet winter morning beside the frozen waters of Lake Minnewanka, as the sun rose over snow and sky, Christopher asked Sandra to walk with him for all the days to come. Through happy tears and joyful disbelief, she said yes.

"I would rather share one lifetime with you than face all the ages of this world alone."

Now, having found their first home and begun shaping their place in the world, they look toward the horizon together. They will continue to explore distant lands, serve their communities, create, teach, and build a life defined not by any single destination, but by the journey they share. With kindness as their compass, love as their constant, and their village growing around them, their greatest adventure has only just begun.

On June 20th, 2026, they begin the next chapter — surrounded by the people they love most.

The Realm Beyond

Explore Moscow & the Palouse

✦

Moscow, Idaho is a hidden gem tucked into the rolling hills of the Palouse — full of trails, markets, culture, and character. Here are some of our favorite spots to help you make the most of your visit.

Nature · Hiking

Idler's Rest Nature Preserve

A beloved forested preserve right in Moscow with winding trails through second-growth cedar forest. The site of our Sunday yoga & hike. Free, open year-round, and one of our favorite hikes near town.
Nature · Hiking · Views

Kamiak Butte County Park

A rocky, pine-topped ridge rising above the Palouse farmland. Hike to the summit for sweeping 360° views across miles of golden wheat fields. About 30 minutes from Moscow — absolutely worth it.
Trees and Gardens · Strolling

U of I Arboretum & Botanical Garden

A serene 65-acre garden ("The Arb") on the University of Idaho campus. Hundreds of labeled tree species from across the globe and peaceful walking paths. Gorgeous in June. Free and open to all.
Wildlife · One-of-a-Kind

WSU Bear Research Center

One of the most unique experiences in the region — observe grizzly bears up close at WSU's research facility. In Pullman, WA (8 miles away).
Campuses · University of Idaho

Explore UI Campus

Walk the historic University of Idaho campus in Moscow. Take a stroll down the Hello Walk, enjoy the architecture, visit the bookstore for Vandal Swag!
Campuses · Washington State University

Explore WSU Campus

Visit Washington State University's beautiful campus in Pullman. Grab ice cream at Ferdinand Shoppe, visit the "Crimson Cube" art exhibits and enjoy the scenery and architecture. Hint: if you see someone in WSU gear and yell "Go Cougs!", they will yell it back. It's the law.
Downtown · Local

Downtown Moscow Shops

Moscow's downtown is lined with independent bookshops, boutiques, galleries, and cafes. Main Street is the beating heart of the city — perfect for wandering, window-shopping, enjoying a morning coffee or evening cocktail.
Market · Saturday Mornings

Moscow Farmers Market

One of the finest farmers markets in the Pacific Northwest — held every Saturday morning in downtown Moscow between 8 am to 1 pm. Fresh produce, artisans, baked goods, and flowers. An absolute must if you're here Saturday morning.
Scenery · Day Trip

Palouse Scenic Drive

Take a drive through the rolling Palouse — one of America's most photographed agricultural landscapes. In June the hills are lush green and gold. Stop at Steptoe Butte for panoramic views for miles.
Adventure · Day Trip

Coeur d'Alene

Famous for its stunning blue waters and the world's longest floating boardwalk, it's the perfect place to grab a coffee, walk the lakefront, or browse the downtown boutiques. If you're flying into GEG or driving down from the north, take a breather in downtown Coeur d'Alene.
Scenery · Day Trip

Elk Creek Falls Hike

Take the scenic 50-mile drive to Elk Creek Falls Trailhead — the highest waterfall in Northern Idaho. Trails range from easy strolls to more moderate loops, making it accessible for anyone wanting a quick "forest fix." Pro-tip: Grab a famous huckleberry ice cream in the town of Elk River on your way back!
Scenery · Day Trip

Giant Cedar Grove

Step back in time at the Giant Cedar Grove Trailhead. This ancient grove features western red cedars that have stood for centuries, including the famous "Champion Tree" — estimated to be over 3,000 years old! The trail is paved and accessible. Just a bit past Elk Creek, so you can easily visit both spots in one trip.

Questions Answered

FAQ

✦
What should I wear?
We invite you to dress in flowy summer dresses, slacks, or button-up shirts — think garden party meets business casual, with an enchanted twist. As our celebration unfolds on open grass in June, please choose footwear suited for a meadow and consider bringing a layer, as the weather can be unpredictable.
Is the venue outdoors?
Yes, the ceremony will be outdoors. June in Moscow can be beautiful but bring a light layer just in case. We'll have contingency plans for rain.
Are children welcome?
The ceremony and reception are for adults only. Please let us know if you might need assistance finding local caretakers for any children you're bringing in your RSVP so we can assist with planning.
Can I take photos during the ceremony?
We ask that guests put phones away during the ceremony so everyone can be fully present. Our photographer will capture everything. Reception photos are very welcome, share away!
Will there be dietary accommodations?
Yes! Please note any dietary restrictions or allergies when you RSVP and we'll make sure you're cared for.
Is there parking at the venue?
Yes, parking is available on site. Carpooling / taking our shuttle from town is highly encouraged to ensure those who need to park at the venue are able to do so.
What do I need for the Sunday yoga & hike?
Bring a yoga mat, a towel, water, and comfortable shoes. The hike is about 1 mile on easy terrain so no special gear is needed!
Who do I contact with questions?
Reach out to Christopher or Sandra directly — they'd love to hear from you.
